About Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects movement. It occurs when nerve cells in the brain that produce dopamine become damaged or die. Early signs of Parkinson's can include tremors, stiffness, and changes in movement patterns. Research has shown that changes in typing patterns may be an early indicator of Parkinson's disease. By studying these patterns, we can potentially identify individuals at risk and provide them with early interventions.

About Our Dataset

Our dataset was collected by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) on two movement disorder units from Madrid, Spain. Subjects were given standardized equipment and were told to type as they usually would at home, free to correct mistakes if they chose to. The first group conducted the study twice with 13 healthy controls and 18 PD sufferers, while the second group conducted the study once with 30 healthy controls and 24 PD sufferers.

Besides the keylogging data (measuring the key pressed, hold time, release time, and press time), the subjects also took finger tapping tests, which measure the speed and rhythm of finger movements, providing additional insights into the progression of Parkinson's disease.
